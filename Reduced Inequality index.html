REDUCED INEQUALITIES

United Nations Sustainable Development Goal 10 (Philippines)

Intro Goal Solutions PH Context Programs Action

Introduction

Inequality persists in income, gender, age, disability, sexual orientation, race, and opportunity. SDG 10 aims to reduce inequality within and among countries by ensuring equal opportunities and eliminating discriminatory laws, policies, and practices.

Goal

The goal of SDG 10 is to empower and promote the social, economic, and political inclusion of all people, irrespective of age, sex, disability, race, ethnicity, origin, religion, or economic status. In the Philippines, this includes addressing income inequality and ensuring equitable access to education, healthcare, and employment.

Solutions

  • Promote inclusive economic growth and equal opportunities.
  • Ensure fair access to education, jobs, and healthcare.
  • Strengthen social protection programs for marginalized communities.
  • Encourage diversity and anti-discrimination policies in workplaces.
  • Support affordable housing and inclusive urban planning.

Philippine Situation

Despite economic progress, inequality remains a major challenge in the Philippines. Gaps between urban and rural areas, and between different social groups, continue to limit opportunities for many Filipinos. Reducing inequality is key to achieving inclusive and sustainable national development.

Government Programs

  • Pantawid Pamilyang Pilipino Program (4Ps): Conditional cash transfer to support low-income families.
  • PhilHealth Universal Healthcare: Expanding healthcare access for all citizens.
  • TESDA and Livelihood Programs: Skills training for employability and inclusion.
  • Anti-Discrimination Ordinances: Promoting equality across gender and social backgrounds.

Take Action

Everyone can contribute to reducing inequalities. Advocate for inclusivity, treat others with respect and fairness, support local communities, and raise awareness about equal rights. Together, we can build a more equitable and just Philippines.

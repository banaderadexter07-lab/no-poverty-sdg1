NO POVERTY

United Nations Sustainable Development Goal 1 (Philippines)

Intro Goal PH Context Programs Solutions Take Action

Introduction: The Human Right

The United Nations established 17 Sustainable Development Goals (SDGs) in 2015 to end poverty, protect the planet, and ensure prosperity by 2030. SDG 1: No Poverty addresses not just income, but access to education, healthcare, and opportunity — the foundation for sustainable growth.

The Goal & Targets

SDG 1 seeks to eradicate extreme poverty everywhere by 2030. Globally, this means helping those living on less than $2.15 a day. In the Philippines, poverty is measured by family income needed for basic needs.

₱13,797 per month (2023)

(Source: Philippine Statistics Authority)

Philippine Context & Challenges

  • Poverty Incidence: 22.4% (2023), affecting 25.24 million Filipinos.
  • Most Affected: Farmers, fisherfolk, and residents of BARMM.
  • Key Barriers: Limited access to education, healthcare, and jobs; vulnerability to natural disasters.

Key Government Programs

  • 4Ps: Conditional cash grants improving health, nutrition, and education.
  • TESDA: Free technical and vocational training for employability.
  • UHC Act: Affordable, quality healthcare for all Filipinos.
  • SLP: Micro-enterprise and job facilitation for marginalized groups.

Sustainable Solutions

  • Boost Local Economies: Invest in rural areas and job hubs.
  • Financial Inclusion: Widen access to banking, credit, and insurance.
  • Climate Resilience: Strengthen protection against natural disasters.
  • Education Access: Improve quality education for long-term stability.

How You Can Take Action

  • Support Fair Trade: Buy local, ethical products.
  • Volunteer: Join educational or livelihood programs.
  • Advocate: Promote equality and anti-corruption policies.
  • Vote Wisely: Support leaders prioritizing poverty reduction.

RESPONSIBLE CONSUMPTION AND PRODUCTION

United Nations Sustainable Development Goal 12 (Philippines)

Intro Goal Challenges PH Context Programs Action

Introduction

Responsible consumption and production aim to ensure sustainable patterns of resource use. SDG 12 emphasizes reducing waste, promoting sustainable business practices, and ensuring that economic growth no longer comes at the expense of the environment.

Goal

The goal of SDG 12 is to promote resource and energy efficiency, sustainable infrastructure, and access to basic services and green jobs. It calls for changing the way we produce and consume goods to reduce waste and environmental damage.

Challenges

  • High levels of waste generation and improper disposal.
  • Overconsumption and reliance on single-use plastics.
  • Lack of awareness about sustainable lifestyle choices.
  • Limited implementation of circular economy practices.
  • Unsustainable production methods in agriculture and industry.

Philippine Situation

The Philippines faces challenges in managing solid waste, with over 40,000 tons produced daily. Many local businesses are now adopting eco-friendly packaging and production methods, but more public awareness and stronger enforcement are needed to reduce pollution and promote responsible consumption nationwide.

Government and NGO Programs

  • Ecological Solid Waste Management Act (RA 9003): Promotes proper waste segregation and recycling.
  • Green Jobs Act of 2016: Encourages industries to adopt sustainable practices and create green employment.
  • Sustainable Consumption and Production Action Plan: Implements strategies for cleaner production and resource efficiency.
  • DENR’s Clean Production Program: Guides companies to reduce emissions and waste during production.

Take Action

You can contribute by avoiding single-use plastics, supporting local eco-friendly products, recycling, and practicing mindful consumption. Every small action counts toward building a sustainable and responsible future for all.
